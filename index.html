Strategic decision framework — when to activate paid
Activate paid placement when...
Impressions are low (<500/week) — paid ads generate meaningful incremental reach that the free listing cannot capture alone.
Future availability is high (2+ rooms) — ample inventory means newly generated demand can actually be monetized.
Weekday / off-peak periods — weekday demand books direct at higher rates, improving net margin on paid clicks.
Longer stays & group bookings targeted — these guest types book direct more often, maximizing ROI on paid exposure.
Pause paid placement when...
Impressions are already high (>750/week) — the hotel is already capturing most available demand; paid clicks add cost without revenue.
Inventory is tight (<1 room available) — ads surface rooms that cannot be sold, generating wasted spend.
Weekend / peak periods — weekend stays skew OTA-prone, reducing direct booking share and net margin on paid traffic.
Always keep the free organic listing ON — zero cost, positive visibility, strengthens direct channel regardless of paid strategy.
Key research findings
Billboard effect
Paid listings influence bookings beyond direct Google-attributed clicks. Searchers exposed to paid ads become more likely to book later through other channels — a platform-native awareness effect that traditional attribution misses.
Direct channel protection
Although paid ads slightly reduce the organic direct booking share, the absolute number of direct bookings still rises because total demand grows. For every incremental booking under PAID, roughly 4 out of 5 occur on the hotel's direct channel.
Future availability metric
GHF advertising triggers on shopping date, not stay date — meaning ads surface for all future dates even when inventory is minimal. The FA metric developed in this study controls for this to distinguish genuine incremental demand from noise.
