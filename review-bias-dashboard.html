How Firm Strategies Affect Consumer Biases in Online Reviews

Service Science, Articles in Advance, February 2023
Cornell University, SC Johnson College of Business
📄 Read Full Paper

🔍 Research Question

How do different review-collection strategies employed by hospitality firms affect the characteristics and biases of online reviews?

Specifically, this study investigates how collection methods impact review ratings, length, polarity, and positivity across major hospitality brands and platforms.

The study analyzes reviews from Marriott, Hilton, Choice, and Wyndham hotels across multiple chain scales (upper upscale, upscale, midscale, and economy) to represent different review-collection strategies in the hospitality industry.

Four Review Collection Methods

Reviews collected via post-stay email survey and posted to hotel's official brand website

Email Invitation Private Survey No Login Review Details

Email Invitation No Survey No Login No Min. Length

💡 Statistical Significance

Random effects models controlling for hotel heterogeneity and temporal factors confirm that collection method significantly impacts ratings (p < 0.001). The Brand method yields approximately 0.2-0.4 points higher ratings compared to self-motivated reviews, while OTA yields roughly 0.1-0.15 points higher.

Major Findings

  • Self-motivated reviews are lowest rated and longest: TA-SM reviews have the lowest average rating (3.86) but provide the most detailed feedback with a median length of 80 words, suggesting more critical and engaged reviewers.
  • Email solicitation increases ratings: All email-prompted methods (Brand, OTA, TA-RE) generate higher ratings than self-motivated reviews, with differences of 0.15 to 0.42 points—statistically and practically significant.
  • Brand collection yields highest ratings but shortest reviews: Brand website reviews have the highest mean rating (4.28) but shortest median length (22 words), potentially due to survey fatigue and pre-existing brand loyalty among direct bookers.
  • Expedia allows minimal effort reviews: 43.6% of Expedia reviews contain no text, only numeric ratings. When text is removed, ratings show even higher positivity, creating potential credibility concerns.
  • Review Express balances volume and detail: TA-RE reviews achieve higher ratings than TA-SM while maintaining moderate length (64 words), potentially reducing nonresponse bias.
  • Platform requirements affect review depth: TripAdvisor's 200-character minimum requirement results in substantially longer, more informative reviews compared to platforms with no minimum (OTA) or limited requirements (Brand).
  • Collection method impacts exceed temporal effects: Collection method effects remain significant even after controlling for hotel characteristics, brand, scale, and time trends, indicating genuine strategic impact.

🎯 Practical Implication

A 0.2-point rating increase (e.g., from 3.9 to 4.1) can lead to significant increases in customer traffic and revenue. For context, previous research shows a half-star improvement on Yelp can increase restaurant reservations by 30-49% during peak hours.

🎓 Theoretical Contributions

Social Exchange Theory Application

The study confirms that review-posting behavior follows social exchange theory: consumers weigh perceived benefits vs. costs when deciding whether to post reviews. Collection strategies that reduce posting costs (email invitations, no login requirements, no minimum length) increase participation but may also reduce review quality and depth.

Self-Selection Bias Insights

The research provides empirical evidence for three types of self-selection bias in online reviews:

  • Polarity self-selection: Consumers with extreme experiences are more motivated to post (especially evident in TA-SM)
  • Purchase self-selection: All methods show positive bias as satisfied customers more likely purchased/completed stays
  • Platform familiarity effects: Collection methods that reduce barriers attract less polarized reviews

Motivation Theory Refinement

Contrary to some prior literature, the study finds that self-motivated (intrinsically motivated) reviewers are less polarized than some email-prompted methods, suggesting that intrinsic motivation in the review context may be more nuanced than previously understood. However, self-motivated reviewers do provide more negative and detailed reviews, consistent with effort-based theories.

💼 Managerial Implications

Strategic Recommendations for Hospitality Firms

1. Balance Collection Methods

Use a portfolio approach to review collection:

  • For higher ratings: Utilize brand website and email-prompted methods
  • For detailed feedback: Encourage TripAdvisor reviews (self-motivated or Review Express)
  • For volume: Leverage OTA platforms, recognizing lower text content

2. Optimize Survey Design

For brands using GSS + review collection:

  • Segment customers: Send private survey to one group, public review invitation to another (avoid survey fatigue)
  • Set minimum word counts: Balance between requiring detail and avoiding abandonment
  • Reduce survey length: Shorter surveys before review requests increase review quality

3. Manage Platform Credibility

Brand websites face credibility challenges due to:

  • Overly positive ratings (82% are 4-5 stars)
  • Short review length (median 22 words)
  • Consumer skepticism about selective posting

Solution: Direct some reviews to third-party platforms (like Hilton/Wyndham do with TripAdvisor) to build trust while maintaining brand website presence for direct bookers.

4. Platform-Specific Strategies

Marriott/Choice approach (Brand + selective TA-RE): Build brand community, maintain control, but may sacrifice TripAdvisor volume and credibility

Hilton/Wyndham approach (TA-RE only): Leverage third-party credibility, achieve higher TripAdvisor volume, potential herd behavior benefits

⚠️ Trade-offs to Consider

Higher ratings vs. Review depth: Methods that generate highest ratings (Brand, OTA) produce shortest reviews with least information for future consumers

Volume vs. Quality: Making reviews easier (OTA approach) increases volume but 44% have no text

Control vs. Credibility: Hosting reviews on brand sites provides control but may reduce consumer trust

🔬 Methodology

Data Collection

Web crawlers collected reviews from:

  • Brand websites (Marriott.com, Choice.com)
  • Expedia.com (OTA platform)
  • TripAdvisor.com (distinguishing Review Express from self-motivated based on "in partnership" label)

Random sampling of ~30 hotels per brand per chain scale level ensured representative coverage across geographic markets and property types.

Analytical Approach

Primary Model: Random effects linear regression controlling for:

  • Hotel-level heterogeneity (random effects)
  • Brand fixed effects
  • Chain scale fixed effects
  • Temporal factors (year and month fixed effects)

Robustness Checks: Hausman test (favored random effects), Breusch-Pagan LM test, Lagrange multiplier tests, fixed effects models, ordered probit models—all confirmed main findings.

Model Specification

Ratingijt = β0 + β1Brandij + β2OTAij + β3TA-REij + Controls + ui + εijt

Where TA-SM serves as baseline, ui captures hotel random effects, and controls include brand, scale, year, and month fixed effects.
