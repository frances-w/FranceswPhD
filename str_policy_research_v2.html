Entry vs. exit dynamics
Entry — persistent deterrence
Months 1-6: -6.46. Months 7+: -6.65.
Immediate and persistent. Compliance costs durably deter new participation.
Exit — temporary spike, then reversal
Months 1-6: +3.5. Months 7+: -2.4.
Initial spike clears non-compliant hosts. Reversal = market restabilizes around resilient incumbents.
Robustness checks
Strategic evasion — three channels tested
Colisting — fewer than 50 properties detected system-wide. Not a material confounder.
Within-listing category switching — listing types stable across all Property IDs. No transitions observed.
Strategic relisting — 0–0.6% of hosts per year. Below 1% in every year.
Pre-pandemic robustness
Pre-COVID sample (Aug 2017 – Feb 2020) confirms negative effect persists at smaller magnitude — regulation drives reduction, not pandemic.
COVID represents common shock — parallel trends preserved across treated and control cities throughout.
Alternative clustering schemes (city-level, two-way) all confirm inference robustness.
