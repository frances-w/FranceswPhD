Research design
Instrumental variable (2SLS)
Charleston's 2018 STR ordinance instruments ln(STR Supply). Policy shock is exogenous — validated via Google Trends showing no demand divergence at policy date.
Two-way fixed effects
City FE + month-by-year FE. Absorbs all location-specific and time-specific confounders. Parallel trends confirmed pre-policy.
Why OLS fails
Airbnb grows in high-tourism markets — same demand that boosts hotels. OLS conflates demand-driven hotel growth with competitive effect, biasing toward zero or positive.

Strategic implications
When Airbnb IS a real threat
Frequent compression markets (>90% occ) — Airbnb absorbs overflow demand hotels could price up
High Airbnb penetration (>15% supply share) relative to hotel stock
Economy and midscale segments — Airbnb's price point competes directly
When Airbnb is NOT a threat
Mid-sized markets without frequent capacity constraints (avg 11 full-compression nights/yr)
Hotels with strong brand loyalty, business travel mix, or service differentiation
Markets where STR and hotels serve distinct traveler segments
Limits of STR regulation
-43% Airbnb supply shock → only -0.06% hotel demand effect. Regulations help hotels less than advocates expect
Zero effect on ADR and revenue — hotels cannot capture pricing upside in low-compression markets
STR and hotels primarily coexist — differentiated market structure persists post-regulation
